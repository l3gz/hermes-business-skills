# Launch Frameworks Reference

This reference summarizes the launch frameworks used by the `launch-email-writer` skill in original wording. It is a field guide, not a source of client-specific facts. Verify every deadline, scarcity limit, price, proof point, and claim against the client's real business before writing.

## Launch Formula Phases

A launch is a sequenced campaign with a story arc, not a single sales email. The phases build anticipation, earn trust through value, sell during a real cart window, and close on a real deadline.

- **Pre-prelaunch:** quietly signal that something is coming. Take the audience's temperature, ask questions, and surface objections early so the prelaunch can answer them. This phase gauges interest without pitching.
- **Prelaunch:** the heart of the campaign. Deliver a series of high-value content pieces that teach, shift beliefs, and build desire before anything is for sale. The audience should get real results from the free content and start to trust the mechanism and the person behind it.
- **Open cart:** announce the cart is open and make the full offer. Anticipation built during prelaunch converts into the first wave of sales.
- **Cart open and mid-launch:** the working middle. Add proof, answer questions, handle objections, share buyer stories, and use interim deadlines such as a fast-action bonus. Keep giving reasons to act now.
- **Close cart:** the real final deadline drives the last and usually largest wave. State the true consequence of missing it.
- **Post-launch:** onboard buyers, follow up honestly with non-buyers, and record what worked for the next launch.

The whole arc depends on anticipation toward a known date and on the genuine value delivered before the sale.

## Prelaunch Content: PLC 1, 2, 3

The prelaunch usually runs as three content pieces, each with a distinct job and each valuable on its own.

- **PLC 1, opportunity and transformation:** answer "why should I care." Show what is now possible, why it matters, and why now. Paint the transformation and open a loop toward the next piece. Handle the objection "this is not for me."
- **PLC 2, transformation and education:** answer "how does this work." Teach something genuinely useful so the audience gets a real win and starts to believe the change is achievable. Show the mechanism. Handle the objection "this will not work for me."
- **PLC 3, ownership experience:** answer "what do I do next." Show what life looks like with the result, introduce the shape of the offer, and set up the cart open. Handle the objection "I cannot do this" or "I do not have time or money," and build anticipation for the cart.

Each piece stands alone as value, addresses a specific objection, and moves the audience one belief closer to buying. Email supports each piece: announce it, deliver it, and follow up on it.

## Sideways Sales Letter

A traditional sales letter makes the whole argument on one page in one sitting. The sideways version turns that letter on its side and spreads it across the prelaunch, so the selling happens gradually through valuable content over days.

Instead of one long pitch, each prelaunch piece carries one part of the argument: the opportunity, then the proof and mechanism, then the ownership experience and the offer. By the time the cart opens, the audience has effectively read the full sales argument, delivered as help rather than as a pitch. The key is that each step gives real value, so the audience keeps opening the next one.

## Mental Triggers

Triggers are psychological levers that make a message more persuasive. Use only the ones that are true for the business, and layer them across the launch so no single email carries all of them.

- **Authority:** people follow credible experts. Use real credentials, results, and demonstrated expertise.
- **Reciprocity:** genuine value given first creates a natural pull to reciprocate. The free prelaunch content earns attention when the cart opens.
- **Community and belonging:** people want to belong to a group and an identity. Show the community the buyer joins.
- **Anticipation:** building toward a known future event is one of the strongest launch triggers. A launch is engineered anticipation toward the cart open and close.
- **Social proof:** people look to others to decide. Use real testimonials, numbers, results, and visible buyer activity.
- **Scarcity:** limited quantity or a closing window motivates action, but only when real. Fake scarcity destroys trust.
- **Likability and story:** people buy from those they like and relate to. The attractive character's honest story and shared values build connection.
- **Event and ritual:** a launch works as a shared, time-bound event with a beginning and an end, which creates its own momentum.

Assign a lead trigger to each email so the sequence builds rather than repeats. Anticipation carries the prelaunch; scarcity and social proof carry the close.

## Perfect Webinar Structure

When a launch runs through a webinar or live training, this structure moves the audience from attention to purchase by breaking the beliefs that block the sale.

- **Introduction and hook:** promise the big outcome, set the one thing they will learn, and give them a reason to stay to the end.
- **The one big idea and origin story:** introduce the new opportunity or vehicle and tell the attractive character's origin story to establish credibility and relatability.
- **The three secrets:** teach through three points that each break one false belief blocking the sale:
  1. The belief about the vehicle or opportunity itself ("this approach will not work").
  2. The belief about themselves ("I could not do this").
  3. The belief about outside obstacles ("something external will stop me": time, money, circumstances).
  Each secret delivers real insight while dismantling one objection.
- **The transition to the offer:** move from teaching to selling by showing that doing this alone is hard and that the offer is the faster, supported path.

## The Stack and the Close

- **The stack:** present the offer as an accumulating list of components, restating the growing total value as each piece is added, before revealing the price. The audience should perceive the full value of everything included before they hear the number, so the price feels small against the stack. Anchor the price against the real value, then add the guarantee and the real scarcity.
- **The closes:** a webinar or launch close uses a series of honest closing angles rather than a single ask, for example:
  - cost of inaction: what staying stuck continues to cost
  - future pacing: what life looks like after the result
  - objection handling: naming and answering the real hesitations
  - decision reframes: the difference between people who act and people who wait
  - urgency and scarcity: the real deadline, bonus, or seat limit
  Each close gives another honest reason to decide now. Never manufacture the deadline, the seats, or the results.

## Webinar Email Support

A webinar launch needs its own email arc around the event:

- **Registration emails:** drive sign-ups with the promise and the date.
- **Show-up and reminder emails:** confirm the time, reduce no-shows, and rebuild anticipation right before the event.
- **Live or "doors open" email:** the cart or link goes live during or right after the event.
- **Replay emails:** deliver the replay with a real replay deadline, then use the close-cart sequence as the replay window ends.

## Launch Email Sequence Minimum

A usable launch plan should include:

- business, offer, avatar, launch type, and real cart-open and cart-close dates
- the phase map with dates
- the prelaunch content plan when used
- the webinar structure and its email support when used
- the mental trigger assigned to each email
- each email with subject options, one call to action, a goal, and the objection or belief it handles
- a close-cart sequence built on the real deadline
- a post-launch plan for buyers and non-buyers
- unknowns and claims needing review
