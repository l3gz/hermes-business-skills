# Attractive Character Profile Questionnaire

> Detailed questionnaire framework for the attractive-character-builder skill.

Attractive Character Profile Questionnaire
1. Business and Audience Foundation
1. What business, offer, product, service, or movement is this Attractive Character representing?
2. Who is the dream customer or dream follower?
3. What painful problem does this audience want solved?
4. What result, transformation, identity, or future do they deeply want?
5. What do they currently believe about this problem?
6. What do they currently misunderstand?
7. What are they tired of hearing from everyone else in the market?
8. What kind of person would they naturally trust?
9. What kind of person would they naturally ignore, resist, or distrust?
10. What do you want this audience to feel when they encounter the character?
* “This person gets me.”
* “This person has been where I am.”
* “This person knows something I need.”
* “This person is fighting for people like me.”

12. Why is this person the right face of the brand?
13. What personal connection does this character have to the audience’s problem?
14. Has this character personally experienced the problem, solved the problem, studied the problem, or interviewed people who solved the problem?
15. What makes this character believable?
16. What makes this character different from the typical expert, influencer, company, or salesperson in this market?
3. Choose the Identity Type
17. Which identity fits this character best?
Leader
The character has already achieved the result and is guiding others from where they are to where they want to be.
Adventurer / Crusader
The character is on the journey, testing, exploring, discovering, and bringing back useful lessons.
Reporter / Evangelist
The character may not have all the answers yet, but is interviewing, researching, documenting, and sharing what they find.
Reluctant Hero
The character does not want to be put on a pedestal, but discovered something so useful that they feel obligated to share it.
18. Which identity feels most authentic?
19. Which identity would your dream customer trust most?
20. Which identity would make your content easiest to create consistently?
21. What is the character’s one-sentence identity statement?
Example:
“I’m not a guru. I’m a real estate investor who figured out how to buy houses with no money down, and now I’m showing regular people how to do it too.”

33. How does the backstory naturally lead to the product, service, method, or message?
34. Complete this sentence:
“I used to believe __________, but after __________, I discovered __________. Now I help __________ do __________ without __________.”
