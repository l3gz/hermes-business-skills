# Offer Frameworks Reference

This reference summarizes the offer-building frameworks used by the `offer-builder` skill in original wording. It is a field guide, not a source of client-specific facts. Use it to think clearly, then verify all claims, proof, prices, guarantees, and scarcity against the client's real business.

## Value Equation

Perceived value rises when the buyer wants the outcome more, believes achievement is more likely, gets progress sooner, and faces less effort or sacrifice.

```text
Perceived value = (dream outcome x perceived likelihood of achievement) / (time delay x effort and sacrifice)
```

Use the four levers like this:

- **Dream outcome:** clarify what the buyer truly wants. Make it specific, emotionally resonant, and expressed in their language.
- **Perceived likelihood:** make success feel believable through proof, process, credentials, fit criteria, diagnostics, milestones, examples, and support.
- **Time delay:** reduce the wait for meaningful progress through quick wins, shorter setup, clear sequencing, and faster feedback.
- **Effort and sacrifice:** remove friction, confusion, complexity, risk, social discomfort, hidden work, and tradeoffs where the business can honestly do so.

Do not fake any lever. If the result takes time or effort, say so and show how the offer makes the path clearer or more supported.

## Grand Slam Offer Construction

A high-value offer is built from the buyer's desired result and obstacles, not from a random list of features.

1. **Name the dream result.** Define the before and after the avatar wants most.
2. **List obstacles.** Capture every barrier that keeps them from the result: knowledge, tools, support, confidence, money, time, accountability, risk, beliefs, and past failures.
3. **Turn obstacles into solutions.** Convert each barrier into something the offer can provide: lesson, service, template, audit, coaching, setup, tool, community, milestone, or guarantee.
4. **Package the solutions.** Decide what belongs in the core offer, what becomes a bonus, what is excluded, and how the buyer experiences the sequence.
5. **Frame the decision.** Name the offer, explain the mechanism, reverse appropriate risk, anchor value, answer objections, and give a truthful reason to act.

The best offers feel tailored because each component exists for a reason.

## Offer Stack and Bonuses

An offer stack makes value visible. It shows the buyer what they receive and why each item matters.

For each stack item, define:

- what it is
- what problem it solves
- how it is delivered
- when the buyer receives it
- why it increases speed, certainty, or ease
- what value anchor is appropriate

Bonuses should handle objections or accelerate implementation. Common jobs for bonuses:

- reduce setup time
- make the first step easier
- provide templates or scripts
- add expert review
- give examples or case studies
- increase accountability
- reduce perceived risk
- help the buyer make a hard decision

Avoid fake bonus economics. A value anchor can be replacement cost, time saved, expert access, comparable service value, risk reduction, or estimated business impact. If the number is not a real price, do not present it as one.

## Guarantee Taxonomy

Risk reversal should fit the business model and be operationally possible.

- **Unconditional guarantee:** the buyer can request a refund inside a clear window with minimal conditions. Best for simple products, lower delivery cost, or businesses that can absorb refund risk.
- **Conditional guarantee:** the buyer qualifies only after completing required actions or meeting stated criteria. Best when the result depends on buyer participation.
- **Anti-guarantee:** the offer clearly states there is no refund or no result guarantee. Best when delivery is customized, capacity is limited, or the work requires committed participation. It must be paired with clear fit criteria.
- **Implied or performance guarantee:** confidence comes from milestones, service terms, outcome-linked components, progress checkpoints, or transparent delivery standards instead of a simple refund promise.

Always state the window, conditions, exclusions, buyer responsibilities, and claim process. Flag health, income, financial, legal, credential, and regulated claims for review.

## Ethical Scarcity and Urgency

Scarcity means limited quantity. Urgency means limited time. Both must be true.

Ethical scarcity can come from:

- limited seats or client capacity
- calendar availability
- cohort size
- inventory
- application review windows
- team delivery bandwidth
- bonuses that require real labor or scheduling

Ethical urgency can come from:

- enrollment deadlines
- live start dates
- seasonal timing
- price changes that are actually scheduled
- expiring audit, onboarding, or implementation windows
- a strategic reason the buyer benefits from acting before a known event

If there is no real deadline or limit, use cost-of-delay framing instead. Explain what waiting may cost, but do not manufacture pressure.

## MAGIC Naming

MAGIC is a naming checklist, not a rigid formula.

- **M, Magnetic reason why:** the distinctive mechanism, belief, discovery, or method behind the offer.
- **A, Avatar:** who it is for or the identity the buyer wants to step into.
- **G, Goal:** the outcome or transformation.
- **I, Interval:** timeframe, milestone, season, cadence, or constraint, only when true.
- **C, Container:** the form of the offer, such as sprint, system, lab, accelerator, audit, challenge, workshop, protocol, roadmap, or membership.

Generate multiple names, then choose the one that is clearest, most credible, easiest to say, and most aligned with the brand voice.

## Offer Roles

Offers work differently depending on their place in the business model.

- **Attraction offer:** creates the first conversion or relationship. It should be easy to understand, low friction, and directly connected to the next step.
- **Core offer:** delivers the main transformation. It needs the strongest mechanism, proof, stack, risk reversal, and sales argument.
- **Upsell offer:** adds speed, depth, support, scale, access, or implementation after purchase. It should feel like the natural next level.
- **Downsell offer:** gives a simpler, lower-cost, or lower-commitment option when the main offer is not the right fit.
- **Continuity offer:** keeps delivering ongoing value through access, support, maintenance, community, replenishment, coaching, or retention.

Build the offer for its role. A lead magnet should not carry the complexity of a core offer, and a continuity offer should justify why value continues over time.

## Offer Document Minimum

A usable offer document should include:

- business, avatar, offer role, price, and sales motion
- one-sentence offer
- dream outcome and mechanism
- value equation analysis
- obstacles mapped to solutions
- offer stack and bonuses
- guarantee or risk reversal
- scarcity and urgency
- MAGIC name options and recommended name
- price framing
- objections handled
- unknowns and claims needing review
