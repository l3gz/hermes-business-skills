# Customer Research Frameworks

Use these frameworks to deepen avatar research before copywriting.

## Top 20 Percent Customer Analysis

Purpose: identify the customers who are most valuable and easiest to serve, then write copy for them instead of everyone.

Steps:

1. Survey or interview existing customers.
   - Ask why they bought.
   - Ask what problem they wanted solved.
   - Ask what result they got.
   - Ask where they first heard about the business.
   - Ask what content, proof, or referral helped them decide.

2. Sort for best customers.
   - Spent the most.
   - Stayed the longest.
   - Got the best results.
   - Referred others.
   - Were easiest to fulfill.
   - Match the future business direction.

3. Find common factors.
   - Demographics.
   - Psychographics.
   - Business/health/life situation.
   - Trigger event.
   - Buying reason.
   - Channel/source.
   - Content consumed before buying.

4. Define qualifiers.
   - Required traits.
   - Disqualifiers.
   - Minimum readiness.
   - Beliefs they must hold.
   - Problems they must care about.

5. Rewrite messaging and sales path.
   - Speak only to the best-fit customer.
   - State requirements openly when useful.
   - Repel bad-fit buyers.
   - Double down on channels that produced best customers.
   - Recreate the buying journey best customers followed.

## Enter the Conversation in Their Head

Purpose: write from inside the customer's existing mental dialogue.

Ask:

- What are they already thinking about the problem?
- What words do they use?
- What are they angry about?
- What are they afraid of?
- What have they already tried?
- What do they blame?
- What do they secretly want?
- What would make them feel understood?
- What would make them skeptical?

Rule: do not start with what the business wants to say. Start with what the customer already feels.

## F.R.E.D.

A compact avatar lens:

- Fears: what they worry will happen.
- Results: what they want to achieve.
- Expectations: what they believe or assume about the market, product, price, timing, and themselves.
- Desires: what they emotionally want underneath the surface result.

## Awareness and traffic temperature

### Awareness

- Unaware: does not know the problem.
- Problem aware: feels pain but does not know the solution.
- Solution aware: knows possible solutions but not this offer.
- Product aware: knows this product but is not convinced.
- Most aware: wants it and needs urgency, proof, or a reason to act now.

### Traffic temperature

- Cold: does not know the brand and may not trust the mechanism.
- Warm: knows the brand, character, category, or problem.
- Hot: already wants the result or offer.

Use colder traffic for education, story, and belief-shifting. Use hotter traffic for direct offer and urgency.

## Evidence labels

- Customer quote: direct language from customer.
- Market signal: competitor/public research.
- Internal assumption: founder/team view.
- Inference: agent synthesis.
- Unknown: not enough evidence.

Never present inference as customer fact.
