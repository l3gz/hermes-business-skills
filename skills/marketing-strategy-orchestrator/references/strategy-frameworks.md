# Marketing Strategy Frameworks Reference

This reference distills the channel-selection and strategy frameworks used by the `marketing-strategy-orchestrator` skill. It draws from two primary sources:

1. **$100M Leads** (Alex Hormozi) — the "Core Four" channel framework and the lead-collector amplification layer
2. **Acquisition.com $100M Scaling Roadmap** — the business-stage progression that determines channel readiness
3. **$100M Offers** (Alex Hormozi) — the pricing and value-framework that determines CAC viability

**Conflict resolution:** Where sources conflict, Hormozi's principles win. Use this as a field guide, then verify against the client's actual business data.

## The Core Four (Primary Acquisition Channels)

Hormozi's framework from $100M Leads defines four primary ways to get prospects. Every business uses some combination of these. The skill's job is to rank them for the specific client.

### 1. Warm Outreach

**Definition:** Reaching out to people who already know you — your personal network, past clients, current customers, followers, email list, LinkedIn connections, community members.

**How it works:** You contact them directly (DM, email, text, call, in-person) with an offer or invitation. Because they already know you, the trust barrier is low and response rates are high.

**When it fits:**
- You have an existing network or audience (even a small one)
- The offer is high-ticket or relationship-based (B2B services, coaching, consulting)
- You need revenue fast and can't wait for content or ads to compound
- You're at Stage 0-1 (no proven funnel yet)
- The avatar buys through trust and personal recommendation

**Cost:** Free (time only). The cheapest channel.

**Speed:** Fastest. You can message people today and get responses today.

**Limits:** Finite. You will exhaust your warm network. Warm outreach is a launch channel, not a scale channel. Once the network is tapped, you need another channel.

**Metrics:** Response rate, meeting booking rate, close rate. Typical warm outreach response rates: 20-40% (vs 1-5% for cold).

**Common mistakes:**
- Not reaching out at all (pride, fear of rejection)
- Pitching instead of starting a conversation
- Not tracking who was contacted and when
- Burning the network with a bad offer (the offer must be good before you spend warm network capital)

### 2. Posting Free Content

**Definition:** Creating and publishing content publicly — social posts, videos, podcasts, articles, blogs, newsletters. One-to-many distribution. Free to post, but costs time and creativity.

**How it works:** Each piece of content is a "unit of content" (Hormozi's term) — the smallest amount of material needed to hook, retain, and reward attention. Content attracts an audience over time. Some audience members become leads, then customers.

**When it fits:**
- The avatar consumes content on a specific platform (you need to know WHERE they spend attention)
- You can create consistent content (at least 3-5 posts per week)
- You're building a long-term asset (content compounds — old posts keep working)
- Any business stage, but especially valuable for Stage 0-2 businesses that can't afford paid
- The offer benefits from education, authority-building, or demonstration

**Cost:** Free (time and creativity). Can be outsourced later.

**Speed:** Slow. Audience building takes months. The first 90 days produce little. But content compounds — by month 6-12, the audience becomes a reliable lead source.

**Platform selection (match to avatar):**

| Platform | Best for | Avatar profile | Content format |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | B2C, lifestyle, visual brands | Consumers, millennials/gen Z | Reels, carousels, stories |
| LinkedIn | B2B, professional services | Decision-makers, professionals | Text posts, carousels, articles |
| YouTube | Education, tutorials, reviews | Problem-aware searchers | Long-form video, Shorts |
| TikTok | B2C, trend-driven, young audience | Gen Z, young millennials | Short-form video |
| X (Twitter) | Tech, startups, thought leadership | Founders, developers, niche communities | Text threads |
| Podcast | Deep expertise, relationship-driven | Commuters, multi-taskers | Audio, long-form |

**Never recommend a platform without confirming the avatar is there.** "Post on TikTok because it's trending" is not a strategy — it's a guess.

**Metrics:** Followers, reach, engagement rate, profile clicks, DMs, leads from content. Content ROI is indirect — track the path from content → DM → lead → sale.

**Common mistakes:**
- Inconsistent posting (the algorithm punishes inconsistency)
- Selling too hard too early (content earns attention; the offer is revealed later)
- Wrong platform for the avatar
- No lead capture mechanism (content without a CTA is entertainment, not marketing)
- Measuring vanity metrics (likes) instead of business metrics (leads, sales)

### 3. Cold Outreach

**Definition:** Reaching out to strangers who don't know you — cold email, cold DM, cold calls, cold LinkedIn messages. The prospect has no prior relationship with you.

**How it works:** You identify prospects (via a list, a search, a scrape, or a database), contact them with a relevant message, and attempt to start a conversation that leads to a meeting or sale.

**When it fits:**
- The avatar is identifiable and reachable (B2B where you can find decision-makers, localized businesses, specific niches)
- The offer is compelling enough to earn a response from a stranger
- Free content is too slow and warm network is exhausted
- You're at Stage 1-2 (proven offer, ready to scale beyond warm network)
- The sales motion allows for a conversation (sales-assisted or sales-led, not pure self-serve)

**Cost:** Low. Tool costs (email automation, LinkedIn automation, data enrichment) run $50-300/month. Time cost is the main investment.

**Speed:** Medium. Pipeline builds over days to weeks. First meetings typically book within 1-2 weeks of consistent outreach.

**Metrics:** Open rate, response rate, positive response rate, meeting booking rate, cost per meeting. Typical cold email response rates: 1-5%. Cold LinkedIn: 5-15%. Cold calls: 1-3% (high volume required).

**Outreach channels:**

| Channel | Best for | Response rate | Volume capacity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cold email | B2B, volume outreach | 1-5% | High (automation) |
| Cold LinkedIn | B2B, relationship-based | 5-15% | Low (daily limits) |
| Cold call | High-ticket, localized | 1-3% | Medium (requires SDR) |
| Cold DM (IG/X) | B2C, creator economy | 3-8% | Low (manual) |

**Common mistakes:**
- Generic templates (personalization drives response)
- No follow-up sequence (most responses come from touch 3-5)
- Pitching in the first message (the goal of cold outreach is a conversation, not a sale)
- Ignoring deliverability (cold emails landing in spam = zero reach)
- Too narrow targeting (small list + high volume burns the list)

### 4. Paid Ads

**Definition:** Paying platforms for distribution — Meta (Facebook/Instagram), Google, YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn, Pinterest, Snapchat. You pay for impressions, clicks, or conversions.

**How it works:** You create ads (creative + copy), target an audience, set a budget, and the platform distributes your ad. The platform's algorithm optimizes delivery to achieve your objective (sales, leads, traffic).

**When it fits:**
- The offer is proven (converts organically through warm/cold/content first)
- There is budget to fund CAC upfront
- The avatar is reachable on the platform
- The business can handle the lead/sales volume
- You're at Stage 2+ (the Scaling Roadmap transition from free to paid)
- CFA is viable (the business can recover acquisition cost fast enough to reinvest)

**Cost:** Direct spend (budget) + creative production. Minimum viable budgets: $50-100/day for testing, $500+/day for scaling.

**Speed:** Fastest scalable channel. Can go from 0 to 100 leads/day in days once the campaign is optimized.

**Platform selection (match to offer + avatar):**

| Platform | Best for | Offer type | Avatar |
|---|---|---|---|
| Meta (FB/IG) | DTC ecommerce, info-products, lead gen | Physical goods, courses, services | B2C consumers |
| Google | Intent-based capture (search) | Solutions to active problems | Problem-aware searchers |
| YouTube | Education, awareness, VSL funnels | Info-products, SaaS, coaching | Solution-aware learners |
| TikTok | DTC, trend-driven ecommerce | Physical goods, apps | Gen Z, young millennials |
| LinkedIn | B2B, high-ticket, lead gen | Professional services, SaaS | Decision-makers |
| Pinterest | DTC, home, fashion, food | Physical goods | Planners, shoppers |

**Metrics:** CTR, CPC, CPM, CPL, CPA, ROAS, frequency, conversion rate. See `meta-ads-manager` references for Meta-specific benchmarks.

**Common mistakes:**
- Running ads on an unproven offer (Stage 1 business running paid = burning money)
- No Pixel/CAPI (broken conversion signal)
- Micro-budgets ($5/day expecting conversions)
- No creative testing structure
- Choosing the wrong platform for the avatar

## The Lead Collectors (Amplification Layer)

Section IV of $100M Leads covers amplification — once the Core Four are flowing, these multipliers increase lead volume without increasing your personal effort.

These are **Stage 3+ plays.** Do not recommend them before the Core Four are working.

### 1. Customer Referrals (Word of Mouth)

Happy customers bring more customers. The cheapest and highest-quality lead source.

- **Trigger:** deliver an exceptional result, then ask for the referral
- **Mechanics:** referral program, incentive, or simple ask
- **When:** Stage 2+ (you have happy customers to refer)

### 2. Employees

Your team members generate leads through their networks, their content, and their outreach.

- **Trigger:** hire people with networks, incentivize them to generate leads
- **Mechanics:** team-based outreach, employee advocacy programs
- **When:** Stage 4+ (you have a team)

### 3. Agencies

Outsource acquisition to specialists who run your channels for you.

- **Trigger:** the business has budget and the founders want to step back from execution
- **Mechanics:** hire an agency for paid ads, outreach, or content
- **When:** Stage 4+ (you have the budget to justify agency fees)

### 4. Affiliates & Partners

Others promote your offer for a commission. You pay only for results.

- **Trigger:** the offer converts well and the margin supports a commission
- **Mechanics:** affiliate program, partner network, joint ventures
- **When:** Stage 5+ (proven offer, margin supports payout, systems to track and pay)

## The $100M Scaling Roadmap (Business Stage Framework)

The Acquisition.com scaling roadmap defines 10 stages (0-9). Each stage has specific challenges and channel implications. The orchestrator skill uses this to determine whether a business is ready for paid, referrals, agencies, or affiliates.

### Stage 0: Improvise
- **State:** No product, no customers, figuring it out
- **Focus:** Find your first customer. Any way possible.
- **Channels:** Warm outreach only. You don't have a product to advertise.
- **Goal:** Get one paying customer to prove the concept.

### Stage 1: Monetize
- **State:** First product, first customers, proving the offer
- **Focus:** Make the offer work. Prove it converts.
- **Channels:** Warm outreach + cold outreach + free content. **No paid ads yet.**
- **Goal:** Consistent organic conversions. If the offer doesn't sell through outreach, it won't sell through ads.
- **Critical principle:** "If you can't sell it to one person, you can't scale it with ads."

### Stage 2: Advertise
- **State:** Offer proven, ready to pour fuel on the fire
- **Focus:** Turn on paid acquisition. Scale what works.
- **Channels:** Add paid ads to the proven organic channels.
- **Goal:** Profitable paid acquisition (ROAS > break-even). CFA viable.
- **Transition trigger:** You've gotten 10-20+ customers organically. The offer converts. Now scale it.

### Stage 3: Stabilize
- **State:** Ads working, but operations are chaotic
- **Focus:** Build systems. Stabilize delivery, fulfillment, support.
- **Channels:** Refine paid, add retargeting, build the funnel, start email retention.
- **Goal:** Predictable, stable acquisition and delivery.

### Stage 4: Prioritize
- **State:** Too many leads, need to focus on best customers
- **Focus:** Filter. Qualify. Focus on the segments that convert best.
- **Channels:** Optimize paid (shift budget to best audiences), build qualification, add referral program.
- **Goal:** Higher quality leads, not just more leads.

### Stage 5: Productize
- **State:** Need a second product for existing customers
- **Focus:** Cross-sell, upsell, retention.
- **Channels:** Email retention, cross-sell campaigns, affiliate program.
- **Goal:** Increase LTV to fund higher CAC.

### Stage 6: Optimize
- **State:** Scaling, need efficiency
- **Channels:** Full-stack optimization. Agency or in-house team managing all channels.

### Stage 7: Categorize
- **State:** Defining market position
- **Channels:** Brand campaigns, market education, category creation.

### Stage 8: Specialize
- **State:** Dominating a niche
- **Channels:** Vertical-specific campaigns, partnerships, acquisitions.

### Stage 9: Capitalize
- **State:** Market leader
- **Channels:** Full dominance — paid + content + referrals + affiliates + agencies + acquisitions.

## Customer Financed Acquisition (CFA) Viability

CFA is Hormozi's framework for determining whether a business can fund upfront customer acquisition costs. It is the financial gate for paid ads.

### The CFA formula

CFA is viable when:
- The business recovers acquisition cost within a short window (days to weeks, not months)
- The gross margin per customer covers the CAC plus operational costs
- The business can reinvest recovered CAC into more acquisition (compounding growth)

### CFA viability test

| Factor | Viable | Not viable |
|---|---|---|
| Gross margin per customer | High (>60%) | Low (<30%) |
| Time to revenue | Immediate (purchase) | Delayed (long sales cycle) |
| Time to CAC recovery | < 30 days | > 90 days |
| Offer type | One-time purchase, subscription, high-ticket | Low-margin commodity, long free trial |
| Backend / LTV | Has upsell or continuity (increases allowable CAC) | Single purchase, no backend |

**If CFA is not viable, do not recommend paid ads.** Focus on free channels (outreach, content) until the offer economics improve.

### How to improve CFA viability
- Increase price (improves margin)
- Add a backend offer (increases LTV and allowable CAC)
- Add continuity/subscription (recurring revenue increases LTV)
- Reduce cost of delivery (improves margin)
- Add an upsell at point of purchase (improves immediate revenue per customer)

## Channel Selection Decision Matrix

Use this matrix to rank channels against the client's inputs. Rate each channel High/Medium/Low on each dimension, then weight by the client's priorities.

| Dimension | Warm Outreach | Free Content | Cold Outreach | Paid Ads |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Speed to first results** | High (days) | Low (months) | Medium (weeks) | High (days, once set up) |
| **Cost** | Free (time) | Free (time) | Low ($50-300/mo tools) | High (budget + creative) |
| **Scalability** | Low (finite network) | Medium (compounds over time) | Medium (limited by list/tool limits) | High (scales with budget) |
| **Lead quality** | High (warm = trusting) | Medium (self-selected) | Medium (depends on targeting) | Variable (depends on creative + audience) |
| **Best for stage** | 0-1 | 0-2 | 1-2 | 2+ |
| **Best for offer type** | High-ticket, relationship | Education, authority | B2B, identifiable avatar | DTC, ecommerce, proven offers |
| **Audience requirement** | Existing network | Platform where avatar is | Identifiable + reachable | Avatar on the platform |
| **Capacity requirement** | Low (manual) | Low (create + post) | Medium (outreach volume) | High (fulfill the volume) |
| **CFA requirement** | None | None | None | Must be CFA viable |

## The Engagement Rule

**Strategy first. Execution second. Approval between.**

The orchestrator skill produces the strategy. The client approves it. Then the sub-skills execute. Never combine strategy and execution in the same step — the client must see and approve the channel mix before any campaigns, scripts, or copy are built.

If the client says "just start building," resist. The strategy must be documented and approved first. A strategy document prevents scope creep, misaligned expectations, and wasted execution on the wrong channels.
