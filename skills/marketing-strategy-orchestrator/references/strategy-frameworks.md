# Marketing Strategy Frameworks Reference

This reference distills the channel-selection and strategy frameworks used by the `marketing-strategy-orchestrator` skill. It draws from three primary sources:

1. **$100M Leads** (Alex Hormozi) — the "Core Four" channel framework, the lead-collector amplification layer, warm/cold outreach playbooks, the Rule of 100, content monetization
2. **Acquisition.com $100M Scaling Roadmap** — the business-stage progression with per-stage channel evolution, metrics, and named tactics
3. **$100M Offers** (Alex Hormozi) — Customer Financed Acquisition (CFA) viability

**Conflict resolution:** Where sources conflict, Hormozi's principles win. Use this as a field guide, then verify against the client's actual business data.

## The Core Four (Primary Acquisition Channels)

Hormozi's framework from $100M Leads defines four primary ways to get prospects. Every business uses some combination of these. The skill's job is to rank them for the specific client.

### 1. Warm Outreach

**Definition:** Reaching out to people who already know you — your personal network, past clients, current customers, followers, email list, LinkedIn connections, community members.

**When it fits:**
- You have an existing network or audience (even a small one)
- The offer is high-ticket or relationship-based (B2B services, coaching, consulting)
- You need revenue fast and can't wait for content or ads to compound
- You're at Stage 0-1 (no proven funnel yet)
- The avatar buys through trust and personal recommendation

**Cost:** Free (time only). The cheapest channel.

**Speed:** Fastest. You can message people today and get responses today.

**Limits:** Finite. You will exhaust your warm network. Warm outreach is a launch channel, not a scale channel.

### 2. Posting Free Content

**Definition:** Creating and publishing content publicly — social posts, videos, podcasts, articles, blogs, newsletters. One-to-many distribution.

**When it fits:**
- The avatar consumes content on a specific platform
- You can create consistent content (at least 3-5 posts per week)
- You're building a long-term asset (content compounds)
- Any business stage, but especially valuable for Stage 0-2 businesses that can't afford paid

**Platform selection (match to avatar):**

| Platform | Best for | Avatar profile |
|---|---|---|
| Instagram | B2C, lifestyle, visual brands | Consumers, millennials/gen Z |
| LinkedIn | B2B, professional services | Decision-makers, professionals |
| YouTube | Education, tutorials, reviews | Problem-aware searchers |
| TikTok | B2C, trend-driven, young audience | Gen Z, young millennials |
| X (Twitter) | Tech, startups, thought leadership | Founders, developers |

**Never recommend a platform without confirming the avatar is there.**

### 3. Cold Outreach

**Definition:** Reaching out to strangers who don't know you — cold email, cold DM, cold calls, cold LinkedIn messages.

**When it fits:**
- The avatar is identifiable and reachable (B2B, localized businesses, specific niches)
- The offer is compelling enough to earn a response from a stranger
- Free content is too slow and warm network is exhausted
- You're at Stage 1-2

**Cost:** Low ($50-300/month tools + time).

**Speed:** Medium (days to weeks for pipeline).

### 4. Paid Ads

**Definition:** Paying platforms for distribution — Meta, Google, YouTube, TikTok, LinkedIn.

**When it fits:**
- The offer is proven (converts organically first)
- There is budget to fund CAC upfront
- The business can handle the lead/sales volume
- You're at Stage 2+

**Cost:** Direct spend + creative production.

**Platform selection (match to offer + avatar):**

| Platform | Best for | Offer type |
|---|---|---|
| Meta (FB/IG) | DTC ecommerce, info-products, lead gen | Physical goods, courses |
| Google | Intent-based capture (search) | Solutions to active problems |
| YouTube | Education, awareness, VSL funnels | Info-products, SaaS |
| LinkedIn | B2B, high-ticket, lead gen | Professional services |

## The Lead Collectors (Amplification Layer)

Section IV of $100M Leads. These multipliers increase lead volume without increasing personal effort. Stage 3+ plays only.

1. **Customer Referrals** — happy customers bring more customers
2. **Employees** — team members generate leads through their networks
3. **Agencies** — outsource acquisition to specialists
4. **Affiliates & Partners** — others promote your offer for a commission

## The $100M Scaling Roadmap (Business Stage Framework)

| Stage | Name | Channel implication |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Improvise | Warm outreach only — find first customers manually |
| 1 | Monetize | Warm + cold outreach + free content. No paid yet. |
| 2 | Advertise | Add paid ads. The transition from free to paid. |
| 3 | Stabilize | Refine paid, add retargeting, build the funnel |
| 4 | Prioritize | Optimize paid, filter leads, build qualification |
| 5 | Productize | Cross-sell, upsell, retention campaigns |
| 6+ | Optimize → Capitalize | Full-stack: paid + content + referrals + affiliates + agencies |

**The critical transition is Stage 1 → Stage 2.** Do not run paid ads until the offer is proven.

## Customer Financed Acquisition (CFA) Viability

CFA is viable when the business recovers acquisition cost within a short window (days to weeks), the gross margin covers CAC plus operational costs, and the business can reinvest recovered CAC into more acquisition.

| Factor | Viable | Not viable |
|---|---|---|
| Gross margin per customer | High (>60%) | Low (<30%) |
| Time to revenue | Immediate | Delayed (long sales cycle) |
| Time to CAC recovery | < 30 days | > 90 days |
| Backend / LTV | Has upsell or continuity | Single purchase, no backend |

**If CFA is not viable, do not recommend paid ads.**

## Warm Outreach Playbook

From $100M Leads. The fastest, cheapest channel. Default first channel for Stage 0-1 businesses.

### The "Do You Know Someone?" Framework

The most effective warm outreach does not ask the contact to buy. It asks if they know someone who fits. The contact self-selects if interested.

**Template:** "By the way, do you know anyone who [struggle] is looking to [dream outcome] in [timeframe]? I'm taking 5 case studies for free. I help them [dream outcome] without [effort]. It works. I guarantee [outcome] or I work with them until they get it. I just worked with [name] who [dream outcome] even though she [same struggle]. Does anyone come to mind?"

**Why it works:** you're not asking them to buy, the contact self-selects, social proof is embedded, and it's easy to forward.

### The 9-Word Email (Dean Jackson)

The simplest reactivation email for a cold list. No images, no links. One question:

**"Are you still looking to [4-word desire]?"**

Responders (5-15% of a cold list) are engaged leads. Non-responders filtered without burning the list.

### Price Escalation Rule

Raise the price every 5-10 customers when starting with free/discounted offers:
- First 5: free (for testimonials)
- Next 5: 50% off
- Next 5: full price
- Next 5: 20% premium (testing ceiling)

### Conversion Benchmarks

- 100 warm contacts → ~20 responses → ~4 take free offer → ~1 paid customer
- Baseline warm close rate: ~1% of initial list
- Revenue math: 500 contacts/week × $400 product = $104K/year

## Cold Outreach Playbook

From $100M Leads. The "advanced cousin" of warm outreach — same one-to-one mechanics, but for strangers.

### The 3 Problems of Cold Outreach

1. **No way to contact them** (the list problem)
2. **They ignore you** (the attention problem)
3. **They're not interested** (the relevance problem)

### Problem 1: The List

Three-tier list-building ladder:
- **Scraping software:** automated extraction from LinkedIn, Google Maps, directories ($50-200/month)
- **List brokers:** pre-built lists of decision-makers ($0.10-1.00/contact)
- **Manual:** search and qualify one by one (time only, highest accuracy)

### Problem 2: The Attention

**Personalization principle:** cold outreach should feel like warm outreach. Find 1-3 things a friend would know about the prospect and reference them.

**"Give the Farm" strategy:** offer something people *currently pay for*, for free. Not "so good they should pay" — "stuff they are paying for right now." The most powerful cold outreach hook.

### Problem 3: The Relevance

**Cold-call script structure:**
1. Personalized opener (reference something specific)
2. Genuine compliment (specific, not generic)
3. Social proof (similar client + result)
4. Soft pitch (the free value)
5. Appointment booking (specific time, not "when are you free")

**Claimed result:** upgrading lead magnet quality increased response rates 3x.

## The Rule of 100

From $100M Leads ("Core Four on Steroids"). The execution discipline framework.

### The Rule

Do 100 core actions per day for 100 consecutive days. No exceptions.

| Channel | The 100 action |
|---|---|
| Warm outreach | 100 contacts/day |
| Content | 100 min creation/day, post ≥1/day |
| Cold outreach | 100 contacts/day |
| Paid ads | 100 min ad creation/day, run daily for 100 days |

### The 3 Amplifiers (More → Better → New)

1. **MORE (volume):** increase quantity of what's working
2. **BETTER (optimization):** improve quality (test one variable at a time)
3. **NEW (expansion):** add platforms/audiences/channels

**Sequencing:** More → Better → New. Always. Most businesses jump to NEW before maximizing MORE and BETTER.

### The "Constraints" Concept

Identify the funnel step with the biggest drop-off. A small improvement there produces the biggest overall gain. Example: doubling a 5% application rate doubles the entire funnel output, while doubling opt-in rate only adds 30% at the top.

## Content Monetization Framework

From $100M Leads. How to turn free content into revenue without burning the audience.

### Give:Ask Ratios

| Platform | Ratio |
|---|---|
| TV | 3.5:1 |
| Facebook/Instagram | 4:1 |
| Growth platforms (TikTok, YouTube) | 5:1+ |

### "Give Until They Ask"

Give publicly, ask privately. Post valuable content. When audience members reach out, move private and make the offer. Lets the audience self-select when to buy.

### Two Ask Methods

1. **Embedded CTA:** soft CTA inside value content, maintaining the ratio. Template: "I'm looking for 5 [avatars] to help achieve [dream outcome] in [timeframe]. DM me [keyword]."

2. **Intermittent ask:** 10 give posts, then 1 dedicated ask post.

## Per-Stage Channel Evolution

From the ACQ $100M Scaling Roadmap. Deeper than stage names.

### Stage 0-1: Warm + Free Credits
- Tell warm network. Use free Google/Facebook ad credits. No paid from operating budget.

### Stage 2: Daily Habit + Test-Then-Double-Down
- **Rule of 100** commitment. Test channels, double down on what works. Avoid early diversification.
- **Named tactics:** CLOSER method (sales conversion), Epiphany Script (testimonials), Angry Boat (upset customers)
- Track actions vs. results

### Stage 3: Trust-Building Content
- Email/content sequences for longer sales cycles. Content nurtures while the funnel handles fast buyers.

### Stage 4: Niche Down
- Symptom: too many unqualified leads
- Fix: better content for the *right* avatar, faster follow-up, raise prices, say "no" to non-fits
- Metrics: calls→close rate, lead response time, CSAT

### Stage 5: LTV Extension
- Symptom: qualified leads getting expensive
- Fix: upsell ladder to second product, increase LTV to fund higher CAC
- Key metric: LTV:CAC (target 3:1+)

### Stage 6: Ad Creation at Scale
- Symptom: ads underperform with cold audiences, CAC rising
- Fix: creative volume at scale, sales-team training, cohort churn tracking

## The Engagement Rule

**Strategy first. Execution second. Approval between.**

Never combine strategy and execution in the same step. The client must approve the channel mix before any campaigns, scripts, or copy are built.
