# Human Editing Rules

Use this reference as the finishing-pass checklist after a draft has already been built from avatar, attractive character, offer, hooks, and brand voice.

## Core rule

Do not invent strategy. Edit the draft that exists.

If the draft is missing avatar, offer, proof, or CTA, flag the gap instead of pretending the edit can fix it.

## Editing principles

### 1. Simple reading level
- Prefer short, familiar words.
- Replace abstract terms with concrete words.
- Split long sentences.
- One idea per sentence when possible.

### 2. Active voice
- Prefer "We help farmers restore soil" over "Soil restoration is helped by us."
- Keep the actor visible.
- Use passive voice only when the actor is unknown or irrelevant.

### 3. Present tense and direct action
- Prefer present-tense copy when it makes the message more immediate.
- Replace vague CTAs with clear action.
- Use specific verbs.

### 4. Remove filler and redundancy
Cut words that do not change meaning:
- really
- very
- actually
- basically
- in order to
- due to the fact that
- it is important to note
- as you may know

### 5. Remove AI slop
Watch for:
- generic hype
- corporate filler
- over-polished transitions
- symmetrical lists that sound artificial
- phrases like "unlock your potential" unless the brand actually uses them
- exaggerated certainty

### 6. Preserve character voice
Do not make every draft sound like the same writer.

Preserve:
- signature phrases
- level of polish or roughness
- rhythm
- humor
- enemy language
- belief language
- CTA style

### 7. Keep claims honest
Flag risky claims:
- health or supplement claims
- income or ROI claims
- legal or financial advice
- credentials
- testimonials or results without proof

Use safer wording when proof is missing:
- "may help" instead of "will cure"
- "designed to" instead of "guaranteed to"
- "customers have reported" only when a source exists

### 8. Strengthen structure
A good edit improves flow:
- hook
- problem
- stakes
- insight or mechanism
- proof
- offer or next step
- CTA

If structure is missing, mark the gap.

## Output modes

### Light edit
Fix clarity, grammar, flow, and obvious AI tone. Keep structure intact.

### Strong edit
Rewrite for punch, rhythm, voice, and conversion. Preserve meaning.

### Diagnostic edit
Return issues first, then rewrite only after the user approves or asks.

## Final check

Before returning edited copy, ask:

- Is the meaning preserved?
- Is the avatar still clear?
- Is the character voice preserved?
- Is the CTA stronger?
- Are unsupported claims safer or flagged?
- Does it sound like a real person wrote it?
