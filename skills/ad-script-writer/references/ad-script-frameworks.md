# Ad Script Frameworks Reference

This reference summarizes the ad script frameworks used by the `ad-script-writer` skill. It is a field guide distilled from the indexed sources (Ad Script Writer v4 notebook, covering Becker TRT, Iman Gadzhi VSL, Cardone cold-call, Jeremy Haynes Hollywood, Sam Ovens Perfect Webinar, Hormozi Hook-Retain-Reward, GaryVee Jab-Jab-Jab, and ad creative testing methodology). Use it to structure the script, then verify all claims, proof, prices, and guarantees against the client's real offer.

## Named Frameworks

### 1. Alex Becker's TRT (Testimonial-Repeat-Testimonial)

**Core psychology:** Sell the click as a low-resistance micro-commitment by generating extreme intrigue, mystery, and FOMO around a "new opportunity," backed by a massive guaranteed bet and a dominant stack of recognizable industry testimonials.

**Structure (beat by beat):**

1. **The Curiosity Frame:** Open with a thumbnail or first frame featuring an unusual, mysterious visual object (a black USB drive, a strange device) close to the lens. This opens an unanswered loop the viewer must resolve.
2. **The Big Bet / Bold Guarantee Headline:** Pair the visual hook with a massive, high-stakes guarantee that challenges the viewer directly: "Your ads scale, or you don't pay."
3. **The Mysterious "New Opportunity" Promise:** State a massive, quantifiable benefit framed around a novel mechanism — never present the solution as something the audience has seen before. Instead of "how to make money on Shopify," frame it as "a Chinese import loophole 7-Eleven has been abusing."
4. **The Authority Testimonial Stack:** Rapidly drop names and logos of highly successful, recognizable figures who currently use the product (Tony Robbins' agency, ClickFunnels, Alex Hormozi) to construct instant credibility.
5. **The FOMO and Specific Pain Agitator:** Induce FOMO by identifying exact real-life financial losses (e.g., wasting $15,000 out of $100,000 on ads) to show competitors are pulling ahead.
6. **The Micro-Commitment Click CTA:** Explicitly explain that you will not explain everything in the ad. Sell the click as a low-friction micro-commitment, promising the full breakdown on the landing page.

**When to use:** Platform: Facebook and Instagram. Industry: SaaS, marketing agencies, B2B, high-ticket info-products. Awareness: Cold, unaware, or problem-aware.

**Example:** Becker's Hyros ad — holds up a thumb drive, guarantees Hyros' AI tracking will lower ad costs by 52%, stacks Dan Henry / Alex Hormozi testimonials, calls out the pain of wasted ad spend, and tells viewers to click for the detailed breakdown.

### 2. Iman Gadzhi's 4-Phase Attention-Control VSL-Ad Funnel

**Core psychology:** Progressively escalate a lead's commitment through a coordinated multi-ad sequence that captures control of their messaging environment, builds authority through system-blaming, and shifts their identity from consumer to buyer before the high-ticket pitch.

**Structure (the funnel's ad sequence):**

1. **Phase 1 — Nurture and Pre-Sale Ads:** Build brand authority through long-form YouTube content (leveraging Google's 7-11-4 Rule: 7 hours of engagement across 11 touchpoints in 4 locations before purchase) and high-production event trailers that capture email and phone registration.
2. **Phase 2 — Reprogramming Ads (Cinematic Spectacle):** Release 2-3 cinematic video episodes that describe the audience's problems better than they can, systematically blaming external factors (smartphone dopamine traps, a "rigged school system") to validate past failures and position the brand as a trusted mentor.
3. **Phase 3 — Attention-Control Thank-You Ad:** Upon registration, immediately direct leads to a thank-you VSL that commands them to join a private, high-engagement channel (WhatsApp group, SMS list) to control the communication environment and drive show-up rates.
4. **Phase 4a — Low-Ticket Filter Ad ($75):** Present a low-ticket offer framed as a vehicle for faster, prioritized implementation. This shifts the lead's identity from "casual consumer" to "buyer," breaking purchase resistance.
5. **Phase 4b — Resistance Downsell Ad ($45):** If they decline the $75 offer, immediately present a $45 downsell, reframed not as a discount but as a "safer entry point" to capture price-sensitive buyers without devaluing the brand.
6. **Phase 4c — High-Ticket Close VSL-Ad ($2,000+):** Present a cinematic closing ad that introduces overwhelming testimonials, curriculum walk-throughs, identity-based selling (Spectators vs. Operators), and a tight 3-hour fast-action scarcity window.
7. **Phase 5 — Self-Destruction:** Delete all workshop videos from the channel to create absolute scarcity, prevent audience fatigue, and prepare for the next cycle.

**When to use:** Platform: Meta Ads, YouTube, email, WhatsApp. Industry: E-learning, digital education, coaching, premium training. Awareness: Moves cold traffic from initial curiosity to high-ticket purchasing readiness.

**Ad script note:** Each phase produces a different ad script. The skill produces each ad individually, but they must be planned as a coordinated sequence.

### 3. Grant Cardone's 10X Cold-Call Script

**Core psychology:** Break through obscurity with immediate maximum energy, establish a high-status professional presence, qualify quickly on real-life pain, and manufacture absolute urgency for a short appointment.

**Structure (beat by beat, under 90 seconds):**

1. **The Minimalist Greeting (0:00-0:05):** Open with your first name only and a high-status office association: "Hi, this is Joe from the office of Grant Cardone." Avoid last names, generic company names, and conversational filler ("How are you today?") — these trigger immediate defensive sales walls.
2. **The 20-Second Reason and Big Claim (0:05-0:20):** State the reason for calling and deliver a massive, quantifiable benefit: "Mr. Cardone asked me to call and give you a tool that has increased sales at companies like yours by 40%."
3. **Rapid Qualification (0:20-0:45):** Ask targeted questions to expose recurring problems and confirm value:
   - The Problem Question: "How many salespeople do you have? What are the two biggest recurring problems?"
   - The Magic Question: "If I could snap my fingers and solve this, what would that look like?"
   - The Money Question: "Why would you spend money on solving this?"
   - The Decision-Maker Filter: "Other than yourself, who else would be involved in this decision?"
4. **The Appointment Proposal (0:45-0:60):** Propose a short, exact time block (18 minutes) and always offer today as the primary option to create instant urgency. Never reveal open availability.
5. **The Lock Close (0:60-0:90):** Solidify the commitment: "Do you have a pencil handy? Write this down. Would there be any reason you wouldn't be able to show up at that time?"

**When to use:** Platform: Cold outbound calls or warm lead follow-up. Industry: Real estate, B2B sales, agency services, professional consulting, high-ticket startups. Awareness: Cold, unaware, or disconnected prospects.

**Objection handling posture:** Operate from the conviction that your product is worth 10 times what you charge. The first caller captures 50% of the market; the last to follow up captures the rest. Anticipate: Not interested, Need to talk to a spouse, Let me think about it, Leave me information, Don't have time, Don't have the budget, Bad time, Price is too much.

### 4. Jeremy Haynes' Hollywood Belief-Shift Framework

**Core psychology:** Shift the prospect's deeply held, failing belief system by using vulnerable storytelling to describe common pain points, then plant a new, empowering belief that naturally aligns with the high-ticket solution.

**Structure (beat by beat):**

1. **Relate to the Old Belief:** Explicitly state the outdated, standard, or failing belief the audience currently holds: "I used to believe that starting a digital agency meant cold calling 100 business owners a day."
2. **Describe the Struggle:** Walk through the painful actions, frustrating blocks, and walls they keep hitting because they operate under that old belief: "I spent 8 hours a day getting hung up on, staying broke, hitting a wall."
3. **The Epiphany Moment:** Introduce a sudden realization — a mentor, a podcast, a book, or hitting rock bottom — where everything clicked: "Then I had an epiphany while listening to a podcast."
4. **Plant the New Belief:** State the new, empowering belief that reshaped their paradigm: "The most successful agencies run engagement campaigns to let prospects know, like, and trust them first."
5. **New Actions and Winning Outcomes:** Detail the successful results, scaling, and freedom unlocked by taking actions rooted in the new belief: "Now my agency generates six-figure months because my actions are aligned with reality."

**When to use:** Platform: Facebook and Instagram video ads (engagement campaigns). Industry: High-ticket consulting, agency services, coaching, premium personal brands. Awareness: Problem-aware leads who do not yet understand why their current actions are failing.

### 5. Sam Ovens' Perfect Webinar Promo Framework

**Core psychology:** Position the webinar as a highly exclusive, high-commitment educational "promised land" by sharing a vulnerable rags-to-riches story, highlighting distilled insights, and demanding a significant time commitment to filter out low-intent browsers.

**Structure (the promo ad script, beat by beat):**

1. **The Numerical Success Invitation (Hook):** Open with an exact, highly desirable, quantifiable benefit: "I want to invite you to an exclusive webinar this week where I show you exactly how to get 30 to 50 high-ticket consulting clients every single month."
2. **The Hook / Target Avatar:** State explicitly who the webinar is for and who should skip it to establish professional exclusivity: "This is strictly for people who want to scale their agency but are trapped trading time for money."
3. **The Vulnerable Origin Story:** Tell a relatable rags-to-riches story: "I started in my parents' garage years ago, broke, running into walls."
4. **The Distillation Hook:** Explain that you have spent your life testing systems and running into walls, and have boiled everything down into three key lessons.
5. **Preview the Three Key Pillars:** Enumerate three non-obvious, actionable takeaways:
   - Pillar 1: A specific, non-obvious solution
   - Pillar 2: The operational mechanism
   - Pillar 3: How to execute for rapid momentum
6. **High-Commitment CTA:** Direct the viewer to reserve their seat while mandating they block off a massive time window (2 to 2.5 hours): "Click below to reserve your seat, but please block out a full 2.5 hours. If you aren't prepared to invest that time, please do not register."

**When to use:** Platform: Long-form Facebook ads and landing page video scripts. Industry: High-ticket B2B programs, consulting, coaching, premium online education. Awareness: Problem-aware to solution-aware.

**Webinar full presentation note:** The Perfect Webinar promo ad drives registration. The full webinar presentation (origin story, three pillars, stack build, price drop, 60-minute countdown close) is a VSL-adjacent format. If the client needs the full webinar script, use `vsl-writer` for the presentation and this skill for the promo ad.

### 6. Alex Hormozi's Hook-Retain-Reward Framework

**Core psychology:** Eradicate the standard give-and-take marketing dynamic by flooding cold scrollers with immediate, frictionless, high-value resources, forcing deep reciprocity, trust, and engaged buyer behavior.

**Structure (beat by beat):**

1. **The Hook (0:00-0:03):** Stop the scrolling viewer using shock or surprise (an unexpected statistic), a provocative question, or a bold promise: "Most business owners waste 80% of their ad budget and don't even know it."
2. **The Retention Loop:** Keep the viewer engaged by telling high-momentum stories, cutting all verbal fluff, and opening curiosity loops (promising to reveal a specific formula later): "Later in this video, I'll show you the exact template we use to audit budgets."
3. **The Reward:** Over-deliver by giving away a highly valuable, immediately actionable asset — a Google Doc, template, calculator, or course — upfront with zero friction or email gating: "Click the link below to download our exact $150M tracking template. No emails, no payment, completely free."

**When to use:** Platform: Short-form video (TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts), email headers, blogs. Industry: Lead generation, SaaS, coaching, direct response services. Awareness: Cold, unaware scrollers you want to warm up instantly.

### 7. Gary Vaynerchuk's Jab-Jab-Jab-Right Hook Framework

**Core psychology:** Build deep emotional equity, platform-native familiarity, and human trust through value-first "jabs" so that when the direct "right hook" CTA arrives, the audience is primed to convert.

**Structure (the multi-ad sequence):**

1. **Deliver Native Jabs:** Consistently publish native, highly visual, humorous, or educational content that offers genuine value, entertainment, or community care with zero sales pitch. These are individual short-form ads or posts.
2. **Launch the Anchor Video:** Deploy an iconic, long-form storytelling video that establishes the brand's identity, mission, and human origin story.
3. **Retarget Engaged Segments:** Track platform analytics to isolate viewers who consumed a substantial portion (50-75% or 2-3 minutes) of the anchor video. Build a custom audience from them.
4. **Execute the Right Hook:** Deliver a highly direct, mobile-optimized CTA with clear pricing and simple buying routes — served only to that warm, engaged segment.

**When to use:** Platform: Facebook and Instagram. Industry: Ecommerce, small businesses, lifestyle products, personal brands. Awareness: Moves cold, unaware prospects to product-aware buyers over a multi-touch sequence.

**Ad script note:** This is a campaign-level framework, not a single-ad structure. The skill produces each jab and the right hook as individual scripts, but they must be planned as a coordinated sequence.

### 8. PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)

**Core psychology:** A universal direct-response structure that names the avatar's problem, amplifies the pain, and presents the solution as relief.

**Structure:**

1. **Problem:** Name the specific pain in the avatar's language: "If you're a [role] struggling with [specific problem]..."
2. **Agitate:** Amplify the cost of the problem — emotional, financial, time, identity: "And every month you don't fix it, it costs you [specific loss]."
3. **Solve:** Present the offer or mechanism as the solution, then deliver the CTA: "That's why we built [mechanism]. Click to see how it works."

**When to use:** Any platform, any industry. Best for 15-30 second video ads where the message must land fast. Works for cold and warm traffic.

### 9. BAB (Before-After-Bridge)

**Core psychology:** A tight three-beat structure that contrasts the current pain state with the desired future, then presents the mechanism as the bridge.

**Structure:**

1. **Before:** Show or describe the current pain state: "Right now, you're [specific frustration]."
2. **After:** Show or describe the desired future: "Imagine [specific transformation]."
3. **Bridge:** Present the mechanism: "[Product] gets you from [before] to [after]. Click to start."

**When to use:** Ecommerce, simple offers, short video ads (15 seconds). Works best when the before-and-after contrast is visually demonstrable.

## Hook Patterns — First 3 Seconds

The hook earns the next 3 seconds. Choose by awareness level.

### Visual hooks

- **The Unanswered Curiosity Loop:** Feature a person holding an unusual, mysterious physical object close to the lens (a black USB drive, a strange device). Opens an immediate loop the viewer must resolve.
- **Aesthetic Authority:** Establish authority visually before saying a word — high-end environment, calm confident delivery, background music that maintains emotional tension. The viewer assumes the content is valuable.
- **The No-Fluff Benefit Headline:** Place a massive, quantifiable benefit directly above the "See More" button on mobile: "Hyros lowers ad costs by as much as 52%. Here's how."
- **Pattern Interrupt Visual:** An unexpected visual — dropping something, pointing at the camera, a sudden movement — that breaks the scrolling pattern.

### Verbal hooks

- **The Shock or Surprise Hook (Hormozi):** Lead with an unexpected statistic that disrupts assumptions: "Most people are wasting 80% of their marketing budget — and they don't even know it."
- **The Provocative Question (Hormozi):** Ask a question that activates self-interest: "What if you could double your revenue without spending an extra penny?"
- **The Bold Promise (Hormozi):** Lead directly with a highly desirable, massive benefit: "Learn how I built a seven-figure business in under 12 months."
- **The Bold Bet / Guarantee (Becker):** Slap an aggressive, high-stakes performance guarantee on the table: "Our tracking AI will scale your ads 20% or you don't pay, period."
- **The Quantifiable Promised-Land Invitation (Ovens):** Open with a specific, numerically validated promise: "I'll show you exactly how to get 30 to 50 high-ticket consulting clients every month."
- **The Minimalist Outbound Greeting (Cardone):** First name only and a high-status reference: "Hi, this is Joe from the office of Grant Cardone."

### Pattern interrupts and curiosity gaps

- **The "New Opportunity" Framing (Anti-Dismissal):** The brain dismisses what it thinks it has seen. Present the mechanism as a completely new opportunity or loophole, never a standard solution. "A Chinese import loophole" instead of "how to make money on Shopify."
- **The Denominator Disruption (Value Equation):** Most hooks promise a bigger dream outcome (the numerator). A powerful interrupt focuses on the denominator — promising to eliminate time delay and effort entirely.
- **The Belief-Shift Epiphany Hook (Haynes):** Relate to the viewer's current failing belief and introduce an epiphany that shattered it, unlocking a new action plan.

### Matching hooks to awareness level

```
[ COLD / UNAWARE ]
  → Visual curiosity loops, shock hooks, value-first jabs, belief-shift stories
[ WARM / SOLUTION-AWARE ]
  → Quantifiable promised-land, target exclusivity, system-blaming, mechanism teases
[ HOT / PRODUCT-AWARE ]
  → Direct right hooks, fast-action scarcity, risk reversal, proof-led hooks
```

**Cold traffic objective:** Stop the scroll, warm them cheaply, force a low-resistance micro-commitment (click or watch). Let the creative do the targeting — track who watches 50-75% to filter engaged prospects.

**Warm traffic objective:** Nurture, educate, build authority. Pair the promised-land invitation with exclusivity (who it's for and who should skip it).

**Hot traffic objective:** Close the sale. Deliver a direct, mobile-optimized CTA with clear pricing, served only to engaged segments.

## CTA Structure

### Direct vs. indirect CTAs

- **Indirect CTA (Selling the Click):** For cold traffic video ads. The click is a low-resistance micro-commitment. Do not explain the product in the ad — instruct the viewer to click for the full explanation on the destination page.
- **Direct CTA (The Right Hook):** For hot traffic and retargeting. A direct, easily comprehensible CTA aimed at converting a sale. Mobile-optimized, clear pricing, frictionless buy path.
- **Behavioral Identity CTA (Gadzhi):** For multi-phase funnels. Layered CTAs that filter traffic — indirect (join the group) → low-ticket ($75) → downsell ($45) → high-ticket ($2,000). Each CTA shifts the prospect's identity toward buyer.

### CTA anatomy

Every CTA must answer three questions:

1. **What do I do?** The action verb: "Click the link," "Reserve your seat," "Book your call."
2. **What happens next?** The immediate result: "You'll be taken to a secure checkout," "You'll get an email with the template."
3. **Why now?** The reason to act immediately: "Bonuses expire in 3 hours," "Only 10 spots remain," "Every day you wait costs you [specific loss]."

Weak CTAs ("learn more," "click here") do not answer these questions and kill conversion.

### Urgency and scarcity in the CTA

- **Urgency (time limits):** Webinar countdown clocks (Sam Ovens uses a 60-minute sidebar timer), fast-action windows (Gadzhi's 3-hour bonus window), outbound urgency (Cardone's "today first" appointment proposal).
- **Scarcity (quantity limits):** Limited seats based on real capacity, real cohort start dates, real bonus availability.
- **Ethical rule:** Scarcity and urgency must be based on real constraints. Never fabricate countdowns, limited spots, or expiring bonuses. If no real deadline exists, use cost-of-delay framing instead.

### Risk reversal in the CTA

- **The Bold Guarantee / Bet (Becker):** Structure the headline around a high-stakes performance challenge: "Scale your ads or you don't pay." Forces attention because the viewer assumes you could not make such a claim if it were not true.
- **The Service-Based Guarantee (Hormozi):** For high-ticket offers, replace money-back guarantees with a performance promise: "We will work with you for free until you hit [X result]."
- **The Consolation Bonus (Hormozi):** When a prospect rejects a CTA on a sales call, never drop the price. Instead, add a high-value bonus that directly solves the stated objection.

### CTA placement by format

| Format | CTA objective | Placement |
|---|---|---|
| Mobile social ads (Meta) | Earn the click | Above the fold — benefit headline + "Here's how" hook above the "See More" button |
| Thank-you / confirmation pages | Environment reassignment | Immediate — VSL commanding the user to join a private group before they close the browser |
| Webinar promos | Registration | End of script — high-commitment RSVP with mandatory time block |
| Cold calls | Secure appointment | Final 30 seconds — short time block, today first, lock close |
| Short-form content | Deliver frictionless value | Immediate or end of video — reward delivered with zero gating |

## Ad-to-Funnel Mapping

Broken message continuity between ad creative and landing pages is a primary cause of funnel failure.

### The three pillars of message match

1. **Aesthetic match:** Visual styling, color schemes, and design atmosphere must remain identical from ad to page.
2. **Language match:** Tone of voice, terminology, and key copywriting phrases must carry through.
3. **Promise match:** The core hook, headline, and promised outcome of the ad must be the primary focus of the page.

If the ad leverages a specific, numerically validated promise, the landing page must immediately zero in on that exact outcome to validate the click.

### Selling the click vs. selling the offer

The ad's sole objective is to sell the click — treating the click as a low-resistance micro-commitment. The ad hooks curiosity, establishes authority, and validates pain so the prospect enters the landing page highly receptive. Do not attempt to explain and sell the entire product inside the ad.

### Hook continuity and expectation setting

- **Eliminate surprise friction:** If the conversion vehicle requires heavy time investment (a 2-hour webinar), tell users to block off that time during registration. Honest expectation setting filters low-intent browsers and primes the remaining audience.
- **Reassign the environment:** Thank-you pages are not dead space. Use them to move leads into a private, high-attention channel (WhatsApp, SMS) to maintain engagement before the core training.

### Behavioral filtering and identity transition

- **The narrow lead magnet:** Solve a narrow, specific obstacle that naturally reveals a deeper problem only the core offer can solve.
- **Low-ticket buyer qualification:** Use a low-ticket gate ($75 or $45) to separate active buyers from casual browsers. Once a user makes a small financial commitment, their identity shifts from passive viewer to active participant, lowering friction for the backend high-ticket offer.

## Ad Creative Testing Methodology

### What to test (isolating variables)

- **The Hook (first 3 seconds and visuals):** Test visual curiosity loops, mobile headlines (bold bets above "See More"), and verbal hooks (shock, question, promise). The hook is the highest-leverage variable.
- **The Body:** Test different actors/creators for demographic matching, and monitor drop-off metrics (watch time, bounce rate) to isolate where the body fails to retain.
- **The Offer and Value Equation:** Test adjustments to the value equation variables — especially the denominator (time delay and effort).
- **The CTA:** Test indirect (sell the click) vs. direct (sell the offer) CTAs, and different urgency framings.

### How many variations to test

- **Vast segmentation (GaryVee):** Split the audience into 20-100+ distinct segments with contextual creative tailored to each. Produce high volume rather than relying on a handful of static creatives.
- **The testing ladder:** Test cheaply first (tweet or blog post), then image, then short clip, then high-production anchor video. Scale production only as the concept proves itself.
- **Unified ad sets (Becker):** Run many variations within a single ad set, all following the same winning template, to isolate individual creative differences.

### Scaling winners

Traditional scaling (increasing budget on a working ad) fails because broader reach targets lower-intent audiences, ballooning CAC.

- **Gadzhi's Double-Down Model:** Identify the lowest-CPL creatives, duplicate them (do not scale budget directly), deploy micro-variations (new hook, new opening frame, new caption), and ensure all budget increases are supported by creative expansion, not hope.
- **Haynes' Creative Family Micro-Tests:** Run a unified creative family with daily micro-tests using variable isolation and budget bucketing.

### Creative fatigue signals and kill criteria

- **Primary fatigue signal:** Rising CAC or severe drop in conversion performance when expanding budgets.
- **Tracking:** Do not rely solely on native platform dashboards (frequently inaccurate). Use direct-attribution tracking to verify which ads generate real sales, not just cheap clicks.
- **Kill rule:** When CAC rises beyond the target threshold and micro-variations do not recover performance, kill the creative family and start a new concept.

## Conversion Psychology Principles

- **Loss aversion:** Frame the cost of inaction as a loss. People feel losses 2x as intensely as equivalent gains.
- **Social proof sequencing:** Start with relatable proof (similar avatar), build to aspirational proof (best result), end with consensus proof (many people like you).
- **Specificity converts:** "Lowered ad costs by 52%" beats "lowered ad costs." Specific numbers, timeframes, and mechanisms build credibility.
- **The Zeigarnik effect:** Open loops (unanswered questions, incomplete stories) hold attention. Close them later in the script or on the landing page.
- **Future pacing:** Have the viewer imagine the after state in sensory detail before the CTA.
- **The "new opportunity" principle:** The brain dismisses what it thinks it has seen. Frame the mechanism as novel to bypass the dismissal filter.
- **Identity-based selling (Gadzhi):** Divide the audience into two groups (Spectators vs. Operators) and force the prospect to choose the identity they want.

## Risk Flags and Claim Safety

Ad scripts carry high claim risk because the claims are spoken, recorded, promoted at scale, and subject to platform review. Flag for review:

- Income or earnings claims ("make $X," "replace your salary")
- Health, medical, or supplement claims
- Guaranteed results without conditional language
- Credential or award claims
- Comparative claims against named competitors
- Testimonial specificity that exceeds the source material
- Bold guarantees that the business cannot operationally deliver

Use conditional language ("results vary," "typical results," "not a guarantee") where appropriate, and route risky claims to the client for legal review before the ad goes live. Platform policies (Meta, TikTok, YouTube) have specific restrictions on claim language — verify compliance before production.
