# Hormozi Ad Blackbook Reference

Distilled from the ACQ Advertising Handbook: "My 20 Best Ad Frameworks of All Time" by Alex Hormozi (Acquisition.com, 2025, 125 pages). OCR-extracted from the primary source.

This is a field guide of reusable ad creative patterns. Each framework is a proven template that Hormozi used to scale four companies (Gym Launch, Prestige Labs, ALAN, Acquisition.com). Use these to brief creatives, generate variants for testing, and diagnose why an ad is underperforming.

**For the full verbatim ad copy and visual breakdowns of each framework, refer to the original handbook. This reference captures the framework names, mechanics, and application guidance.**

## The Ad Kaleidoscope (Part I)

The creative testing framework. A system for taking a winning ad and spinning dozens of variations without starting from scratch.

### The Principle

Most businesses find a winning ad and then struggle to create the next one. The Kaleidoscope solves this by treating a winner as a *pattern* that can be remixed along specific axes. One winner becomes 50+ variations.

### The 9 Remix Axes

Take a winning ad and vary one axis at a time:

1. **Speed:** change the pace — speed up, slow down, time-lapse
2. **Filters:** apply different visual filters (B&W, vintage, high contrast, warm)
3. **Background:** change the setting (office, outdoors, studio, car, gym)
4. **Fonts/Captions:** change the text overlay style (bold, handwritten, minimal, meme-style)
5. **Headline:** swap the verbal hook while keeping the visual
6. **Medium:** change the format (selfie video → voiceover → text-on-screen → UGC)
7. **Format:** change the aspect ratio or platform-native style (Reel, TikTok, carousel, static)
8. **Spliced Sections:** take sections from different winners and combine them
9. **Effects:** add visual effects (zoom, shake, split-screen, text animations)

### How to Use It

1. Identify your current winning ad
2. Pick one axis to vary (never multiple at once — you lose the signal)
3. Generate 5-10 variations along that axis
4. Test them against the original
5. Winners become new baselines to remix further

This is the systematic alternative to "just make more ads." It ensures every new creative has a reason to exist and a hypothesis to test.

## The 20 Ad Frameworks (Part II)

Organized by the business they were created for. Each framework includes: the ad type, why it works, the visual hook pattern, and how to apply it.

### Section I: Gym Launch Frameworks (B2B, service businesses)

#### 1. Personal Testimonial / Origin Story
- **Mechanic:** builds mystery through an open loop. Claims a ridiculous outcome up front, then shows circumstances that seem to make the outcome impossible (bad location, no money, small size). Stacks "anti-proof" until the loop snaps closed with proof at the end.
- **Why it works:** eliminates objections by showing the outcome happening *despite* every reason it shouldn't. The viewer's specific objection is preempted.
- **Visual hook:** shaky selfie style (creates movement, looks social-native). Pans to show the environment.
- **Apply when:** you have a strong result that seems too good to be true. Show all the negatives first, then the outcome.

#### 2. Prop Comedy
- **Mechanic:** uses a physical prop as a visual pattern interrupt and metaphor. The prop is unexpected, memorable, and creates a mental anchor.
- **Why it works:** props are visual hooks that work without sound. They create curiosity ("what is that?") that earns the watch.
- **Visual hook:** presenter holding or interacting with an unexpected object.
- **Apply when:** your product or mechanism can be represented by a physical object. The prop makes the abstract tangible.

#### 3. Emotional Avatar Testimonial
- **Mechanic:** a real customer tells their story, emphasizing the emotional transformation (not just the practical result). Before-pain → discovery → after-joy.
- **Why it works:** emotional resonance creates identification. The viewer sees themselves in the customer's story.
- **Visual hook:** customer on camera, authentic setting (not studio), emotional delivery.
- **Apply when:** you have a customer with a compelling before-and-after story. Especially powerful for transformation offers (fitness, coaching, finance).

#### 4. Show Don't Tell
- **Mechanic:** demonstrate the product working in real-time without explaining it. The proof is in the demonstration, not the claims.
- **Why it works:** eliminates skepticism. You're not telling them it works — you're showing it.
- **Visual hook:** screen recording, live demo, before-during-after visual.
- **Apply when:** your product produces a visible, demonstrable result. SaaS, physical products, design services.

#### 5. Long Term Result
- **Mechanic:** show a customer months or years after the transformation. The result has lasted. This is the anti-"quick fix" ad.
- **Why it works:** combats the #1 objection to transformation offers: "it won't last." Long-term proof is rare and therefore credible.
- **Visual hook:** customer in their current (post-transformation) life, reflecting on where they were.
- **Apply when:** you have long-term customers with sustained results. Premium for retention-focused offers.

### Section II: ALAN Frameworks (SaaS, software, info-products)

#### 1. "Looking For 5 Avatars..."
- **Mechanic:** direct call-out seeking a specific number of specific avatars for a specific outcome. "I'm looking for 5 [avatars] who want to [outcome] in [timeframe]."
- **Why it works:** scarcity (only 5), specificity (exact avatar), and self-selection. The viewer either is or isn't the target — no wasted clicks.
- **Visual hook:** direct-to-camera, simple setting, conversational tone.
- **Apply when:** you have a high-ticket or coaching offer with limited capacity. The scarcity must be real.

#### 2. New vs. Old
- **Mechanic:** contrast the old way (painful, slow, expensive) with the new way (your product). Side-by-side or sequential comparison.
- **Why it works:** the contrast makes the value obvious. The viewer feels the pain of the old way and the relief of the new.
- **Visual hook:** split-screen, before/after, or "stop doing X, start doing Y" format.
- **Apply when:** your product replaces an existing, painful process. Especially strong for SaaS and productivity tools.

#### 3. "Why You Will Never..."
- **Mechanic:** contrarian hook that pushes against a common belief. "Why you will never [common goal] by doing [common method]."
- **Why it works:** creates tension with the viewer's current beliefs. If they agree with the common method, the ad challenges them. If they already suspect it's broken, the ad validates them.
- **Visual hook:** direct-to-camera, strong opening statement, text overlay of the contrarian claim.
- **Apply when:** your offer is positioned against a dominant but flawed approach in your market.

#### 4. The "North Star"
- **Mechanic:** anchor the ad to a single guiding metric, principle, or goal that the viewer should focus on. Everything else is noise.
- **Why it works:** simplifies a complex decision. Instead of comparing features, the viewer focuses on one metric that matters.
- **Visual hook:** single number or principle displayed prominently. Everything else is framed around it.
- **Apply when:** your market suffers from decision fatigue and your offer has a clear, measurable differentiator.

#### 5. No BS Selfie
- **Mechanic:** raw, unpolished, direct-to-camera selfie video. No script, no editing, no production value. Just the founder talking honestly.
- **Why it works:** authenticity cuts through polished ad fatigue. Feels like a friend's video, not an ad.
- **Visual hook:** phone-camera selfie, natural lighting, no cuts.
- **Apply when:** your audience is ad-fatigued, your brand is personality-driven, or you're testing a raw angle against polished creative.

### Section III: Skool Frameworks (Community, education, platforms)

#### 1. Trending Format
- **Mechanic:** use the current trending video format/meme/audio on the platform, but adapt it to your offer. Ride the algorithm's preference for trending formats.
- **Why it works:** the platform boosts trending formats. Your ad gets distribution it wouldn't earn with a standard format.
- **Visual hook:** native to the current trend (varies — check what's trending on the platform this week).
- **Apply when:** you can produce content fast enough to catch trends while they're still trending. Requires platform-native fluency.

#### 2. Value Anchor
- **Mechanic:** lead with a high-value piece of information or insight for free. The value of the free content anchors the value of the paid offer that follows.
- **Why it works:** reciprocity. You gave them something valuable, so they're primed to receive the offer.
- **Visual hook:** educational format — whiteboard, screen share, talking head with text overlays.
- **Apply when:** your offer is info-based (courses, coaching, SaaS). The free value must be genuinely useful, not a teaser.

#### 3. Bribe
- **Mechanic:** offer something for free (tool, template, guide, audit) in exchange for engagement or contact info. The bribe is the hook.
- **Why it works:** reduces friction to zero. The viewer doesn't have to buy — they just have to accept a free thing.
- **Visual hook:** the free thing displayed prominently (a PDF, a tool screen, a checklist).
- **Apply when:** you have a lead magnet that genuinely solves a narrow problem. See the Lead Magnet framework in `offer-builder`.

#### 4. Underdog
- **Mechanic:** tell the founder's story from the bottom. Broke, unknown, dismissed — and then the turnaround. The viewer roots for the underdog.
- **Why it works:** emotional identification. Everyone has felt like an underdog. The story arc is universally compelling.
- **Visual hook:** archival footage, old photos, or reenactment of the struggle. Then the contrast with current success.
- **Apply when:** the founder has a genuine underdog story. Must be true — fabricated underdog stories backfire.

#### 5. Challenge With Prize
- **Mechanic:** run a challenge (5-day, 7-day, 30-day) with a prize for completion. The challenge creates engagement; the prize creates urgency.
- **Why it works:** gamification + scarcity + community. Participants invest time, which creates commitment. The prize drives action.
- **Visual hook:** challenge branding, countdown, prize reveal.
- **Apply when:** you can deliver a structured challenge with a clear outcome. Works for fitness, coaching, skill-building, B2B services.

### Section IV: Book Launch Frameworks (Info-products, launches, events)

#### 1. Flying Prop
- **Mechanic:** throw or toss a physical object (book, product, prop) toward the camera. The motion creates an involuntary visual stop.
- **Why it works:** the brain is wired to track moving objects. The toss is impossible to ignore.
- **Visual hook:** the toss itself — usually in the first 0.5 seconds.
- **Apply when:** you have a physical product to show. The toss must be safe and repeatable.

#### 2. Bribe (Book Launch Variant)
- **Mechanic:** same as Skool #3 but adapted for a launch — free chapter, free preview, free companion resource with the book.
- **Visual hook:** the free resource displayed alongside the product.
- **Apply when:** launching a product with a companion freebie.

#### 3. "The Rumors Are True"
- **Mechanic:** open with intrigue — "the rumors are true" — then reveal the product or offer as the thing being rumored about.
- **Why it works:** curiosity gap. The viewer didn't know there were rumors, and now they need to know what they're about.
- **Visual hook:** conspiratorial or excited tone, direct-to-camera, building to the reveal.
- **Apply when:** you have market buzz or can manufacture genuine intrigue around a launch.

#### 4. "You're Being Lied To"
- **Mechanic:** direct accusation — someone or something in the market is deceiving the viewer, and this ad reveals the truth.
- **Why it works:** anger + validation. The viewer already suspects they're being misled. The ad confirms it and offers the alternative.
- **Visual hook:** serious tone, direct-to-camera, text overlay of the accusation.
- **Apply when:** your offer is positioned as the honest alternative to a deceptive market. Must be truthful — false accusations backfire.

#### 5. Data Stack
- **Mechanic:** present a rapid-fire sequence of data points, statistics, or proof elements. Each one builds the case.
- **Why it works:** cumulative credibility. One data point is a claim. Ten data points are a case.
- **Visual hook:** text overlays with numbers, fast cuts, building visual proof stack.
- **Apply when:** you have multiple proof points (results, testimonials, stats, comparisons). Strong for analytical audiences.

## The Production Checklist (Part III)

Hormozi's QA rubric for ad creative. Divided into four parts: Visual, Verbal Delivery, Script, CTA. Each has Must-Haves and Nice-to-Haves.

### Visual Elements

**Must-Haves:**
- Paired verbal & visual hooks (the first 3 seconds must hook both the eyes and the ears)
- Movement (static ads underperform — motion earns attention)
- Similar avatar (the person in the ad should look like the target avatar)
- Show what you tell (if you claim it, show it visually)
- Captions (sound-off viewing is the majority — captions are mandatory)
- Platform native (the ad must look like organic content on that platform)

**Nice-to-Haves:**
- Aspirational setting or trending formats
- Real-world demonstrations and settings (not studio)
- Props (physical objects that anchor the message)

### Verbal Delivery

**Must-Haves:**
- Simple words (3rd-grade reading level — see the Hormozi Writing Rules)
- Enunciation (no mumbling — every word must be intelligible)

**Nice-to-Haves:**
- Details and numbers (specificity builds credibility)
- Continuous open-looped curiosity (keep them watching to find out)
- Benefits and pains through the prospect's real-life experiences

### Ad Script

**Must-Haves:**
- Call out prospects (name them: "if you're a [avatar]...")
- Compelling reason why (why this, why now)
- Comparisons (old vs. new, your way vs. their way)
- Dream outcome AND nightmare of staying the same (dual motivation)
- Speed to result AND delay of inaction (urgency both ways)
- Certainty of new path AND risk of current path
- Ease of result AND difficulty of current path
- As many proof points as possible

**Nice-to-Haves:**
- Damaging admissions (admit a flaw — builds radical credibility)
- Stakes, struggles, investment (show what's at risk)

### CTA

**Must-Haves:**
- One clear verbal & visual next step to take

**Nice-to-Haves:**
- Reasons to act (why now)
- CTA congruent with the hook (the action matches the promise)
- Scarcity and urgency
- Bonus or incentives
