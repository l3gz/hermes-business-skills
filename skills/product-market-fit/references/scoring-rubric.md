# PMF Scoring Rubric (/100)

Score each dimension on its **0–5 band** below, then multiply by the dimension weight and divide by 5 to get points. Sum all points for the total out of 100.

`points = (band_score / 5) × weight`

| # | Dimension | Weight |
|---|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Problem severity & frequency | 20 |
| 2 | Demand & customer sentiment | 20 |
| 3 | Market size & growth | 15 |
| 4 | Competitive gap / differentiation | 15 |
| 5 | Willingness to pay / monetization | 10 |
| 6 | ICP reachability (distribution) | 10 |
| 7 | Timing / tailwinds | 10 |

Each dimension **must** cite evidence. A dimension with no evidence is capped at band 2 and flagged as an unknown (lowers overall confidence).

---

## 1. Problem severity & frequency — weight 20
Is this a painkiller or a vitamin? How often does the pain occur?
- **5** — Acute, frequent (daily/weekly) pain; people already pay or hack workarounds to solve it. Clear "hair on fire" language.
- **3** — Real but occasional pain, or a "nice to have" people tolerate.
- **1** — Mild annoyance; no evidence anyone actively seeks a fix.
- **0** — No evidence the problem exists for real users.

## 2. Demand & customer sentiment — weight 20
What does public sentiment say about the problem and existing solutions? (Inferred, not surveyed.)
- **5** — Strong, repeated demand signals; visible frustration with current options; people asking "is there a tool for X?"
- **3** — Mixed/moderate signal; some interest, some indifference.
- **1** — Mostly indifference or satisfaction with the status quo.
- **0** — Negative signal — people actively don't want this / tried and abandoned the category.

## 3. Market size & growth — weight 15
Is the addressable market big enough and growing?
- **5** — Large, clearly growing market (positive trend data); credible TAM.
- **3** — Moderate or flat market; viable niche.
- **1** — Small or shrinking; hard to scale.
- **0** — No meaningful market / declining to zero.

## 4. Competitive gap / differentiation — weight 15
Is there a defensible wedge? Note: **zero competitors usually means no market**, not opportunity.
- **5** — Healthy competition (validates demand) + a clear, specific gap or underserved segment to own.
- **3** — Competitive but crowded; differentiation possible but not obvious.
- **1** — Saturated with strong incumbents and no clear wedge — OR zero competitors with no evidence of latent demand.
- **0** — Dominated by an entrenched monopoly/free default that fully satisfies users.

## 5. Willingness to pay / monetization — weight 10
Is there evidence people pay (or would pay) for a solution?
- **5** — People already pay for adjacent/inferior solutions; clear price anchor exists.
- **3** — Plausible monetization; some paid comparables.
- **1** — Users expect it free; weak monetization path.
- **0** — No evidence of willingness to pay anywhere in the category.

## 6. ICP reachability (distribution) — weight 10
Can you actually reach and acquire these customers affordably?
- **5** — Tight, well-defined ICP concentrated in reachable channels (specific subreddits, communities, search terms, ad audiences).
- **3** — Definable but diffuse audience; acquisition possible but costly.
- **1** — Vague "everyone" audience or no obvious channel.
- **0** — Effectively unreachable / no viable distribution.

## 7. Timing / tailwinds — weight 10
Why now?
- **5** — Strong tailwind (new tech enabler, regulation, behavioral shift) makes now the moment.
- **3** — Neutral timing; no strong tailwind or headwind.
- **1** — Mild headwind; market not ready or fatigued.
- **0** — Strong headwind / window has closed.

---

## Verdict bands (total /100)
- **80–100 — Strong fit. Pursue.**
- **65–79 — Promising. Pursue with the named refinements.**
- **50–64 — Mixed. Reframe / narrow the ICP and re-test before committing.**
- **35–49 — Weak. Pivot the angle.**
- **<35 — Kill.**

## Confidence rating (report alongside the score)
Based on evidence *quality*, not the score itself.
- **High** — multiple independent, recent, credible sources per dimension; few unknowns.
- **Medium** — decent coverage but gaps in 1–2 dimensions, or some thin sourcing.
- **Low** — several dimensions rely on inference or weak sources; treat score as directional only.

A high score at **Low** confidence is "promising but under-researched" — recommend more validation before committing, not "go."

## Scoring discipline
- Do not round up to clear a band. If it lands at 64, it's Mixed, not Promising.
- If two dimensions score 0–1, the idea cannot exceed "Mixed" regardless of total — name the fatal flaw explicitly.
- Re-state the single biggest risk in one sentence at the top of the verdict.
